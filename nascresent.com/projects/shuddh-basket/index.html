Project Background

Shuddh Basket is a fresh produce supply chain company that aims to bridge the gap between producers and retailers by eliminating the chain of an intermediary. Currently, the supply chain is equipped to move 5 tonnes of perishables from farms to businesses, every day, in less than 12 hours.

My Role

At the initial stage of this project, it was planned to launch the app only in Mangalore city. There were three modules to be designed under this project Shuddh basket for the customer, Shuddh basket for businesses and delivery partner app. I was assigned to design the experience for the customer's app in this project.

Problem

The producers have to depend upon local traders and an intermediary for the disposal of their farm produce which is sold at thrown away prices.

The traditional supply chain is highly inefficient, unorganized and has a high rate of food wastage.

Retailers face problems like higher costs, low quality and unhygienic products at high price volatility and the everyday hustle of going to the market.

Solution

Shuddh Basket is a solution that solves the problem of fresh produce by leveraging innovative technology.

Retailers receive fresh produce at a competitive price that too deliver to the door steps which saves time.

Design Process

Interviews

I did a brainstorming section and formulated the interview protocol with particular objectives for the type of data I was looking for. I then conducted semi-structured interviews with a few numbers of fresh produce farmers and retailers, Here are some of the insights I have got from the interview.

“Book losses due to the low prices of our product”

“High purchase rate because of the intermediary”

“It's time-consuming to find the right producer with the
products that are not only in hygiene condition but
comes with good price”

“Produce goes bad and creates food wastage due to
a lack of market demand.”

“We make Poor decisions due to inadequate information
on the market prices for our product”

Market Research

As a part of market research, I have gone through a couple of grocery apps like Bigbasket, Blinkit, and JioMart to perceive the pattern they follow and ninja cart which is a direct competitor to see how they doing in the current market. With the rapid growth of digital platforms like e-commerce for businesses, it is important for fresh producers to consider the same direction.

“India has 120 million farmers, out of which 30 million
use smartphones and have a basic sense of understanding
about digital marketplaces.”

It is evident that those who have embraced online methods of marketing and selling
have successfully boosted their businesess. and they improve sales and providing
in-depth product information has shown to build trust and convey quality.

User Persona

After getting insights from research, I created personas that represented our potential target users and captured their needs and frustrations.

User Flow

I kept the user flow very minimal so that the product will be ease of use and have better accessibility.



Sketches

I started with some basic sketches to understand what users will see on each screen and the sections they interact with.
